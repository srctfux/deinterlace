About 
 

TV is a piece of software that grabs analog, interlaced video, and deinterlace it to make it a progressive scan feed for use on your computer monitor or projector.

Analog, interlaced video is what output devices like VCRs, Laserdiscs, game consoles, DVD players output

Unlike most existing software that tries to do the same, DScaler implements very sophisticated algorithms to deinterlace such sources. Using such algorithms makes sure that each kind of video source is handled a different way. It provides the user with a level of image quality unknown to PC owners up to now.

Some of those techniques are ;

  • Video source deinterlacing
  • inverse Telecine detection 2:2 (for PAL movies) as well as 3:2 (for NTSC movies) pulldown
  • Filters for noise removal

The image quality resulting from those complex operations is high and allows some people equipped with data capable projectors to feed them with a progressive scan source that was interlaced right before.

The main forum for user discussion is the Home Theater Computers Section on the AV Science Forum.
