dTV Authors 
 

John Adcock

John is the founder of the project. He did huge amounts of full time development on versions 1.0 through 1.6 Beta 4. He did most of the work cleaning, translating and legalizing Multidec. He put into code, the world's first open-source real-time 3:2 pulldown software deinterlacer (based on Mark Rejhon's algorithm article). He also designed an open-source video-material deinterlacer that is superior to those in virtually any other any PC-based video software product.


Mark Rejhon

Mark authored the 3:2 pulldown algorithm that forms the basis of the algorithm implemented into DScaler by John Adcock. He also does other programming tasks like code cleanup, merging, documenting, testing, enhancing of contributors' changes that are submitted to the project. He also enhances DScaler with his own changes to certain parts of the code, including improvements to the deinterlacer modes.


Steve Grimm

Steve is contributing enhancements to the deinterlacing modes including better automatic switching between video mode and 3:2 pulldown mode, and handling of material that has bad 3:2 pulldown. In particular, Steve made significant modifications to the deinterlacing algorithm in version 2.0.0. He also worked on an early version of a 3:2 pulldown deinterlacing algorithm. In addition, he is the author of the aspect ratio autodetection code which was included in version 1.9.


Tom Barry

Tom contributed the triple buffering code and synchronization fixes for version 2.0.0. Also, he has authored several MMX assembly optimizations to the video deinterlace mode and added the advanced Brooktree/Conexant chipset settings menu, both of which were included in version 1.9.


Michael Samblanet

Michael is continuing to upgrade his aspect ratio control code.

Michael Eskin

An engineer working at Conexant Systems Inc., the makers of the 8x8 series chips that DScaler runs on.  He basically meditates peace between the DScaler software and the 8x8 video capture hardware.  When any of us fails on a nasty 8x8 issue, he takes over and gets the job done from time to time.  One of his several valuable contributions include a bug fix to make Macrovision-encoded sources watchable in DScaler, without purple-lines interference.

Laurent Garnier

Despite having really no link with the french DJ, he's the latest one on the DScaler boat. French however, he coded the autodetection of WSS (WideScreen Signalling), improved a lot the OSD with various "status screens". He also did the popup menu, per-format settings persistency. He's the second European coder, along with John Adcock.

 

Please see the Thanks file for an incomplete list of the many others who have contributed time and effort.
